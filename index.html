Why this matters

Many students do not identify their situation as food insecurity. This tool helps normalize support and shows that asking for help is okay.

Dignity first:

Responses are used only to connect students with resources.

Not one-size-fits-all:

Different answers lead to different levels of support.

How It Works

The design keeps the process simple, private, and connected to existing campus supports.

1

Find the site

Students scan a QR code or visit the website from a campus resource page.

2

Answer privately

Students complete a short self assessment without being publicly identified.

3

Get matched

The site recommends meal swipes, snacks, groceries, or follow-up support.

4

Improve access

Student feedback helps improve the site and reduce future barriers.

Help Us Improve

Your feedback helps us understand what students need and how to make food resources easier to access.
